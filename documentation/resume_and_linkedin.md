## Interview Talking Points

Practice saying these answers out loud before your interview:

**"Walk me through this project."**
> "I built a retail operations analytics dashboard that connects sales, inventory, fulfillment, and supplier data into one place. I started by designing a MySQL database with 7 normalized tables. Then I wrote 12 SQL queries to answer specific business questions. Finally I visualized everything in Power BI with 4 pages and 11 DAX measures, plus an Excel version as a secondary dashboard. The whole project was inspired by my real experience working in Walmart retail operations, where those are exactly the decisions managers need to make every day."

**"Why did you choose these tools?"**
> "I chose MySQL because it's one of the most widely used relational databases in the industry and it reinforces my SQL skills. I chose Power BI because it's the leading business intelligence tool in enterprise environments and is directly applicable to most MIS and analyst roles. I added Excel because it's still the most common reporting tool in operations — not every manager wants to open Power BI. And I used Python just for data generation and cleaning, which shows I can use it as a supporting tool without over-engineering."

**"What was the hardest part?"**
> "The data modeling was the most important decision. I had to decide how to structure the tables so they were normalized — no duplicated data — but also practical to query. The biggest choice was putting unit_price in the order_details table instead of just the products table. That's because prices change over time, and if you only store the current price in products, your historical order data would be wrong after any price update. That's a real-world data integrity issue I thought through carefully."

**"How does this connect to your Walmart experience?"**
> "Almost everything in this dashboard maps to something I've done at Walmart. The inventory table with reorder levels mirrors how we track stock in the store. The fulfillment status tracking is exactly what online grocery pickup operations look like — is the order picked, packed, complete, or delayed? The supplier performance queries reflect how we evaluate receiving and restocking. I built this project specifically to show that I already understand these systems — I just added the data and analytics layer on top."
